Hey, I'm Parth Batra.

I teach machines to think for a living, and argue about music for free.

I grew up in Gurgaon—close enough to Delhi to inherit its opinions, far enough to pretend I don't live there — and I've spent about four years now somewhere in the messy middle of data, ai, and the question of what any of it actually means.

These days I'm a Senior ML Engineer at Procol, turning procurement chaos into systems that quietly make decisions. Before that I picked up scars—the kind you only earn by shipping something that breaks at 2am—at Maruti-Suzuki Genpact MunchStudio


I'm rarely still for long. Work keeps me in the air—some months it's three or four flights—but the trips I actually count are the ones I choose: one big escape a month, the higher the altitude or the colder the better. This year alone I rang in the new year in Meghalaya, froze through the Dayara Bugyal trek at −19°C, ran Kashmir and Amritsar back-to-back, disappeared into Spiti Valley, then slowed all the way down for Banaras.

I write here to think out loud—part field notes from the frontier of the work, part hot takes I'd defend over a drink (that "humour is just how you bleed without anyone seeing the wound"). It's unedited on purpose: a journal that wandered into public and decided to stay.


The place has a few rooms...

blog is the long-form stuff, the things I actually sit down to write; quickwits catches the rest first—raw, rapid-fire, straight off my X feed; thegoodstuff is everything I'd vouch for. handpicked; workbench is where half-built side projects and learning logs live; list100 is a century of dreams I'm foolish enough to track in public;

Off the clock I fall down pop-culture rabbit holes, watch X implode with popcorn in hand, and hunt for artists before the algorithm catches on.


The credentials, for the curious: a dual degree from BITS Pilani—B.E. (Hons.) in Mechanical Engineering and M.S. (Hons.) in Mathematics, which is a fancy way of saying I like building things and proving they work. Less officially, I've logged 100k+ minutes a year on Spotify, a statistic I'm arguably prouder of.

For the record, the day job: I call myself a full-stack ML engineer because I'm greedy about the whole lifecycle, not just the part that demos well:

  • data pipelines that survive contact with real data
  • models that earn their keep instead of decorating a slide
  • LLM agents that occasionally make me forget they aren't people
  • SQL elaborate enough to deserve its own changelog
  • microservices and CI/CD that deploy on a Friday without flinching
  • dashboards that say something true, even when it's inconvenient

If any of that sounds like your kind of noise, you know where the writing is.
