Hey, I'm Parth Batra.

I teach machines to think for a living, and argue about music for free.

I grew up in Gurgaon—close enough to Delhi to inherit its opinions, far enough to pretend I don't—and I've spent about four years now somewhere in the messy middle of data, models, and the question of what any of it actually means.

These days I'm a Senior ML Engineer at Procol, turning procurement chaos into systems that quietly make decisions. Before that I picked up scars—the kind you only earn by shipping something that breaks at 2am—at Maruti Suzuki Genpact Brightchamps Munch.

I call myself a full-stack ML engineer because I'm greedy about the whole lifecycle, not just the part that demos well:

  • data pipelines that survive contact with real data
  • models that earn their keep instead of decorating a slide
  • LLM agents that occasionally make me forget they aren't people
  • SQL elaborate enough to deserve its own changelog
  • microservices and CI/CD that deploy on a Friday without flinching
  • dashboards that say something true, even when it's inconvenient

I write here to think out loud—part field notes from the frontier of the work, part hot takes I'd defend over a drink (that "humour is just how you bleed without anyone seeing the wound"). It's unedited on purpose: a journal that wandered into public and decided to stay.

The place has a few rooms. The blog is the long-form stuff, the things I actually sit down to write; QuickWits catches the rest first—raw, rapid-fire, straight off the Twitter feed. The Good Stuff is everything I'd vouch for without an algorithm holding a gun to my head: books, films, music, podcasts. The workbench is where half-built side projects and learning logs live, and List 100 is a century of dreams I'm foolish enough to track in public.

The credentials, for the curious: a dual degree from BITS Pilani—B.E. (Hons.) in Mechanical Engineering and M.S. (Hons.) in Mathematics, which is a fancy way of saying I like building things and proving they work. Less officially, I've logged 100k+ minutes a year on Spotify, a statistic I'm arguably prouder of.

Off the clock I fall down pop-culture rabbit holes, watch Twitter implode with popcorn in hand, and hunt for artists before the algorithm catches on. If any of that sounds like your kind of noise, you know where the writing is.
